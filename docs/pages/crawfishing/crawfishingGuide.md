# Comprehensive Guide to Catching Wild Crawfish  
### *From Habitat to Plate — The Complete Playbook*

---

## I. Location, Timing & Investigation

> **Goal:** Find **untouched, high-yield spots** with **large, active crawfish**.

### A. Prime Habitats — Be a *Crawfish Investigator*

| Habitat Type       | Best Features                                                                 | Pro Tips |
|--------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------|
| **Rivers & Creeks**| Slow eddies, islands, undercut banks                                          | Drop traps **15 ft from shore** |
| **Lakes & Reservoirs** | Rocky + muddy zones side-by-side, ravines, small coves                     | Look for **rock/mud transitions** |
| **Shoreline Clues**| Shells, claws, legs — **no human footprints** = virgin spot                  | Scout early morning |

> **Investigate:** Call **Fish & Game**, bait shops, or check mole mounds. Move if only small crawfish appear.

---

### B. Optimal Timing

| Factor             | Best Conditions |
|--------------------|-----------------|
| **Season**         | **Mid-season (June–Aug)** → largest crawfish active |
| **Water Temp**     | **58°F+** (hibernation below) — up to mid-November in warm rivers |
| **Time of Day**    | **Night = best**, but **modern traps work daytime** (tan color) |

---

## II. Bait Selection & Prep

> **Crawfish are picky.** Fresh + oily = success.

### A. Top-Performing Baits

| Bait               | Why It Works |
|--------------------|--------------|
| **Trout / Salmon / Tilapia / Herring** | Oily, non-predatory — strengthens shells |
| **Tilapia (budget king)** | Cheap, effective, widely available |
| **Coconut**        | Surprisingly potent |

### B. Baits to Avoid (Myths Busted)

| Bait               | Result |
|--------------------|--------|
| Hot dogs, bacon, cat food, dog food | **Low yield** (11 vs 8 vs **trout = 50+**) |
| **Pike, bass, catfish** | **Crawfish flee** — natural predators |

### C. Bait Prep (Max Oil Release)

1. **Keep skin on**  
2. **Cut through head + gills**  
3. **Slice down spine** to expose meat  
4. **Cut into 1–2" chunks**  
5. **Use FRESH — never rot**

## III. Equipment & Trapping

### A. Trap Design Checklist

| Feature               | Must-Have |
|-----------------------|-----------|
| **Entry throat**      | **≥3–4"** for 10–12" crawfish |
| **Color**             | **Tan** (blends with bottom) — **NO black** |
| **Bait cage**         | Central, enclosed — forces entry |
| **Line**              | **550 paracord** or stronger |
| **Capacity**          | **Bigfoot-style (2 ft × 1 ft)** ideal |

### B. Soak Strategy

| Soak Time       | Use Case |
|-----------------|----------|
| **30–60 min**   | **Fast fishing** — stay with traps |
| **Overnight**   | Risk of **cage pirates** |

> **Alternatives:** Snorkel, hand-line, or **night picking** with flashlight

---

## IV. Trap Security & Legal Compliance

### A. Anti-Theft Tactics

| Method                  | How |
|-------------------------|-----|
| **Hide rope**           | Under bushes/rocks |
| **Sub-surface buoy**    | Floats 6–12" below surface |
| **Camouflage line**     | Green/brown paracord |
| **Fast soaks**          | **1-hour pulls** — never leave unattended |

### B. Stay Legal

| Rule                    | Action |
|-------------------------|--------|
| **Tag traps**           | Name + phone (required in many states) |
| **Check local regs**    | Bait, trap size, bag limits |
| **Invasive species**    | **Remove & eat** — do **NOT return live** |

---

## V. Handling, Cleaning & Cooking

### A. Transporting Live Crawfish

| Method                  | Max Time |
|-------------------------|----------|
| **Dry cooler**          | 2–3 hours |
| **Aerated water**       | 1–2 days |
| **Submerged trap**      | 1–2 days (camping) |

### B. Purging — Myth vs Reality

| Method                  | Truth |
|-------------------------|-------|
| **Salt purge**          | **Myth** — causes vomiting, not cleaning |
| **Fresh water rinse**   | Optional — many skip it |
| **Avoid entrails**      | Just don’t eat the digestive tract |

---

### C. Cooking (Field or Home)

#### Boil Recipe (Per 20 lbs Crawfish)

- **Seasoning:** Creole boil mix  
- **Add-ins:** Corn, andouille sausage, lemons  
- **Boil:** **5 minutes**  
- **Steam:** Transfer to sealed cooler + seasoning → **10 min**

#### Freezing Cooked Crawfish

| Method                  | Instructions |
|-------------------------|--------------|
| **Whole**               | Gallon bags, freeze flat |
| **Tail meat**           | Small bags + Creole seasoning |
| **Reheat**              | **Hot tap water soak ×2** (10 min each) — **NO reboiling** |

---

### D. Shelling Like a Pro

| Part         | Technique |
|--------------|-----------|
| **Tail**     | Press **1st rung**, pull tail, **pinch end** → meat slides out |
| **Claw**     | Break off, remove pincher, **fork in backside** |
| **Knuckle**  | Big crawfish = **bonus meat** |
